# Acknowledgements

The editor would first like to thank all the contributors to this textbook for their enthusiasm, dedication, and creativity. Their commitment to embracing the production of this text as part of their studies and making it their own has been truly inspiring. The experience of co-creating this open educational resource has been an extremely rewarding example of open pedagogy—a collaborative and participatory approach to learning that strengthens engagement with science and knowledge. This project has deepened our belief in open pedagogy as the future of education, to which we feel increasingly committed.

Thanks are also due to Professors Anke Moerland and Sjoerd Claessens of Maastricht University for believing in this project from its earliest stages. Michel Saive and Akorshi Sengupta of Maastricht University Library provided invaluable support and expertise in open science; this textbook would not exist without them. The editor and contributors are deeply grateful to Professor Fabian Raimondo of Maastricht University, who generously shared his experience as counsel and advocate before the ICJ during one of our meetings and offered a thoughtful critical reading of an early draft. Finally, heartfelt thanks go to Professor Gleider I. Hernández of KU Leuven, former President of the European Society of International Law (until September 2025), for finding time in his demanding schedule to write the Preface.

All errors and omissions remain the responsibility of the editor and authors alone.

_Monica Garcia-Salmones, Editor_

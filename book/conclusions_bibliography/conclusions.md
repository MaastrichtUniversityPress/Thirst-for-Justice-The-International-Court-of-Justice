# Conclusions

This booklet has traced the ICJ’s origins, and that of its predecessors, the PCA and the PCIJ, the landmark cases that have shaped its jurisprudence, and the significant pending matters that continue to test its role in a rapidly changing world. They show that the ICJ stands as a cornerstone of the international legal order, an institution forged in the aftermath of a global conflict to bring reason, dialogue, and law into the heart of international disputes.

Yet, the ICJ is not without its challenges. Its reliance on state consent limits its reach; compliance with its judgments is not always guaranteed; and its jurisdictional constraints often hinder its ability to respond to pressing global crises. In a world marked by shifting power dynamics, increasing geopolitical tension, and transnational issues like climate change and cyber conflict, the ICJ must adapt to remain relevant and effective. But adaptation does not necessarily mean remodelling or amendment. The question of the modification of the ICJ is not as clear as day. One could also argue that the fact that the Court has managed to adjudicate many extremely political cases has been ensured by its all kinds of technicalities and limitations.

The narrative of the description of some of the ICJ’s most important cases, past and present, that was presented in the previous pages demonstrates the special place that the Court occupies in global legal order. We ascertain that the ICJ has been a beacon of juridical reflexion and restraint that is per se valuable in the often volatile global politics. Moreover, there have been a few extremely successful judgments along it history, in the sense of their capacity to shape minds and policies towards a more rational, humane and just order.

If anything, in our view the Court is barely in the beginning of its history. One can imagine many different ways in which it might become more and more a shaper of a peaceful and just world legal order.

We conclude this booklet with a positive tone, as we think that the future of the international legal order remains promising. The growing recognition of legal norms, the rising participation of states in adjudication, and the increasing visibility of international law in global affairs all suggest that the ICJ’s role is not diminishing, but evolving. As new generations of jurists, diplomats, and citizens continue to champion the rule of law, the Court’s mission endures: to settle disputes peacefully, to uphold justice, and to serve as a beacon of legal integrity in a complex international system.
